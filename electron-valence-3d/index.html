Understanding Quantum Atomic Orbitals

This web workspace is designed specifically to help students and visual learners transition from the simplistic Bohr Shell Model to the actual 3D Quantum Mechanics Model of elements.

How to read the visualization:

  • 3D Orbital Wireframes (New!): Visualizes the exact mathematical outer shell boundaries (spheres for $s$, dumbbells for $p$, clovers for $d$, complex lobes for $f$) corresponding to the real wavefunctions.
  • Probability Cloud Mode: Represents the volume of space around the nucleus where there is a 90% probability of finding the electron ($|\psi|^2$). The particle dots represent quantum state densities.
  • Wave Flow Mode: Represents the classical deterministic path or flow lines of corresponding electron wavepackets inside their probability density lobes.
  • Uncertainty Flash Mode: Models the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle and Copenhagen Interpretation. Instead of a set orbit, the electrons flash in random positions centered on the wave probability field density.

What are Quantum Numbers?

Principal ($n$): Defines the shell layer and energy level of the electron ($n=1, 2, 3...$).

Azimuthal ($l$): Defines the angular momentum shape of the orbital ($l=0: \text{s-spherical}$, $l=1: \text{p-dumbbell}$, $l=2: \text{d-clover}$, $l=3: \text{f-complex}$).

Magnetic ($m_l$): Defines the 3D spatial orientation of the orbital lobe (e.g., $p_x, p_y, p_z$).

Spin ($m_s$): Defines the intrinsic spin direction of the individual electron ($+\frac{1}{2}$ spin up, $-\frac{1}{2}$ spin down).

Hund's Rule and Pauli Exclusion:

Observe how electrons occupy degenerate subshell lobes (like the 3 separate $p$-lobes) singly first with identical parallel spins before pairing up. This interactive model simulates Hund's Rule of maximum multiplicity perfectly in real time!
